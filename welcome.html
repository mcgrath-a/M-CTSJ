Project Description

My project is a short, interactive quiz-like game on micro-feminism inspired by a late-night TikTok (way past my bedtime). It harnesses the power of seemingly small, everyday actions to challenge deep-seated capitalist and misogynistic ideologies.**If you do wish to play the game before reading this entire page, please skip to User flow and navigation guidance to understand how that page works.

Motivation

After class discussions, sometimes I feel a sense of somberness, questioning whether I, as an individual, can genuinely make any significant impact or change in the world—especially when confronting deeply entrenched capitalist, misogynistic, and racist ideologies. Despite hearing and feeling the passion that something needs to be done, I felt my voice or personal agency could not make a difference. What can I do to change the cycle? To combat these feelings of helplessness, I realized that the key was to delve deeper into micro-feminism.

This unique interactive short game, viewed through an Intersectional Feminist lens, creatively disrupts traditional narratives. It demonstrates how individual actions can subvert entrenched power systems when applied in daily P.O.V. scenarios. This game is an educational tool and an invitation to actively engage with feminist theories, connecting them to everyday practices. It aims to bridge the gap between feminist theory and practical application. Reflecting on the TikTok that inspired this game, I'm grateful I stayed up past bedtime.

What is Microfeminism

While writing this text in Google Docs, the word "microfeminism" is flagged by my spell-check as incorrect. When I asked ChatGPT about "microfeminism," it initially responded with limited information, indicating that this term is not widely recognized. However, this concept does exist and has been discussed in various forms. Dating back to at least 2015, one of the earliest mentions I found is in Mira Charlotte Krishnan's blog, where she delves into the nuanced interplay between microfeminism and macrofeminism. Mira Charlotte Jourdan (Krishnan) is a is a social entrepreneur and feminist activist, passionate about the lives of girls and women, early childhood development, and sustainable communities.
In the blog "On Micro- and Macrofeminism," she explores the nuanced interplay between microfeminism and macrofeminism. Mira discusses how both personal and broader societal feminist actions are essential. She points out that micro-feminism involves individual experiences and daily interactions that reflect feminist values, like addressing everyday microaggressions and personal advocacy. Mira’s reflection in her blog emphasizes that true feminist activism should not ignore the small, personal experiences of inequality and misogyny at home because they are foundational to the individual's feminist consciousness. She argues that overlooking these aspects can undermine the authenticity and integrity of one’s feminist stance. More recently, and I correlate this with the term's virality (exponential viralness) in the past months, more articles and Op-eds have discussed micro-feminism; one in particular written by Ann Martin in Her.ie, an online news site targeted for Irish women, is titled “What is micro-feminism? Fighting gender inequality in little ways” discusses the concept of "micro-feminism" as an approach that emphasizes fighting gender inequality through small, everyday actions. This form of feminism is about recognizing and addressing the subtle and often overlooked instances of sexism that occur in daily life, thereby promoting gender equality on a more personal and immediate scale. Martin highlights how these seemingly minor actions can collectively have a significant impact on the broader feminist movement.​

Who and why

This project is designed to interlink the themes and insights from our class discussions with an innovative, interactive format, focusing on the concept of micro-feminism. The intent is to empower players—students, educators, and anyone interested in feminist theories—to realize the significant impact of small daily actions against larger oppressive structures. I hope the innovation of using computer science and crossing that over with feminist theory shows that there are truly no bounds in education, especially regarding the barrier of entry.


User flow and navigation guidance

Each scenario will have a Option A or Option B selection that must be answered to reval the information below it. Using your knowledge or your best educated guess click an option to reval the answer !
At the top of the page is a correct answer count that will tally the number of correct answers you have for the 5 scenarios. Choose wisely because you cannot go back and change your answer!
Towards the end of the text, there is a purple ‘Read More’ button that when pressed opens a ‘pop-up’ with more detailed information and sources that support that scenario.
The ‘Read More’ pop-up contains 2 sections, 1) the elaboration and more analysis of the scenario, and 2) the sources.
Finally, once answering all the 5 questions, you will be met with a nice surprise at the end. Have fun scrolling through a new way to look at microfeminism!
